Your cold email has about 3 seconds to earn a reply. Most web designer emails waste those seconds with generic intros and vague promises.

Here are five templates that work — each designed for a different situation. The key to all of them: be specific, be brief, and give the prospect a reason to respond.

## Template 1: The Preview Pitch

This is the highest-converting template because you're leading with proof, not promises.

**Subject:** I redesigned your website — take a look

> Hi [Name],
>
> I came across [Business Name] and thought your website could better reflect the quality of your business.
>
> So I put together a quick redesign concept: [Preview Link]
>
> No strings attached — just wanted to show you what's possible. If you like the direction, I'd be happy to discuss making it a reality.
>
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** The prospect sees a redesign of *their* website before they spend a dime. It's tangible, personal, and impossible to ignore.

## Template 2: The Local Expert

Best for targeting businesses in your city or region.

**Subject:** Quick thought on [Business Name]'s website

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm a web designer based in [City] and I work with local businesses like yours to improve their online presence.
>
> I noticed a few things on your current site that might be costing you customers — [specific issue: slow load time / not mobile-friendly / outdated design / hard to find contact info].
>
> Would you be open to a quick 10-minute call? I have a couple of ideas that could help.
>
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** The local angle builds trust. Mentioning a specific issue shows you actually looked at their site.

## Template 3: The No-Website Pitch

For businesses you find on Google Maps that have no website at all.

**Subject:** I built a website for [Business Name]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have a website yet, so I put one together using your Google Maps listing: [Preview Link]
>
> It's got your business info, photos, and reviews — all in a professional layout that works on any device.
>
> If you'd like to get it live, I can have it up and running in a few days. Happy to chat whenever works for you.
>
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** You've removed every barrier. They can see exactly what they'd get, with zero effort on their end.

## Template 4: The Competitor Angle

When a prospect's competitor has a much better website.

**Subject:** You're losing customers to [Competitor Name]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I was looking at businesses in [industry] in [City] and noticed that [Competitor Name] has a significantly stronger web presence than [Business Name].
>
> Their site is modern, mobile-friendly, and shows up higher in Google results. Right now, potential customers searching for [service] are more likely to find them first.
>
> I help businesses close that gap. Would you be interested in seeing what a redesigned [Business Name] website could look like?
>
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** Competition is a powerful motivator. No business owner wants to lose customers to a rival.

## Template 5: The Value-First Follow-Up

For prospects who didn't respond to your first email.

**Subject:** Re: [Original Subject]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I reached out last week about your website. I know you're busy, so I went ahead and put together a quick concept: [Preview Link]
>
> It's based on your current site but modernized — mobile-friendly, faster, and designed to convert visitors into customers.
>
> If it's not the right time, no worries at all. Just thought it was worth sharing.
>
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** Following up with a preview instead of a "just checking in" email gives them something new to respond to.

## Tips for all templates

- **Keep it under 100 words.** Business owners skim. Get to the point.
- **Personalize every email.** Use their name, business name, and a specific detail.
- **One call to action.** Don't ask them to visit your portfolio AND book a call AND reply. Pick one.
- **Send Tuesday through Thursday.** Monday is chaos, Friday is checkout mode.
- **Follow up twice.** Most replies come from the second or third email, not the first.
