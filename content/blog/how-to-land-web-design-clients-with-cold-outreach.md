Cold outreach is the fastest way to get web design clients. It's also the fastest way to get ignored.

The difference between freelancers who close deals and those who hear crickets comes down to one thing: **proof**.

## Why most cold emails fail

Here's what a typical freelancer cold email looks like:

> "Hi, I noticed your website could use some improvements. I'm a web designer and I'd love to help you modernize your online presence. Here's my portfolio..."

The problem? Every other freelancer sends the same thing. Business owners get dozens of these emails. There's nothing to differentiate you, and there's no reason for them to reply.

## The "show, don't tell" approach

Instead of *describing* what you could do, **show them**. Generate a redesign of their actual website and send it as a preview link. When a business owner sees their own brand — with their content, their colors, their business — redesigned into something modern, it hits different.

It's no longer a cold pitch. It's a demonstration of value.

## How to structure your outreach

### 1. Find prospects with bad websites

Look for local businesses with outdated websites. Google Maps is a goldmine — search for businesses in your area and check their sites. Restaurants, dentists, plumbers, real estate agents — these industries are full of websites that haven't been updated in years.

### 2. Generate a redesign

Use a tool like PitchKit to paste their URL and generate an AI-powered redesign in minutes. Pick the style direction that fits their brand best. No hours in Figma, no coding required.

### 3. Send the preview with a personalized email

Your email should be short and direct:

- One sentence about their business (show you did research)
- The preview link
- A simple call to action

That's it. The preview does the selling for you.

## What makes this work

When a prospect clicks your preview link and sees a polished redesign of *their* website, several things happen:

1. **They feel understood** — you used their content and brand, not generic mockups
2. **They see the gap** — the contrast between their current site and the redesign is obvious
3. **The barrier drops** — they don't have to imagine what you'd build. It's right there.

## The numbers

Freelancers who send redesign previews instead of traditional cold emails report significantly higher response rates. Even when prospects don't buy immediately, the preview starts a real conversation instead of silence.

## Start today

You don't need a massive portfolio or years of experience. You need one thing: the ability to show a prospect what their website *could* look like. That's the most powerful pitch in web design — and it takes minutes, not hours.
